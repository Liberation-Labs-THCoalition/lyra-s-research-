We present a method for detecting and correcting confabulation in language
models at inference time, requiring no access to model weights, no training
data, and no modification to the model itself. Singular value decomposition
(SVD) of the key-value cache---a standard byproduct of autoregressive
generation---produces spectral features that distinguish genuine reasoning
from confabulation. We introduce Marchenko-Pastur (MP) corrected features
derived from random matrix theory that are approximately
dimension-invariant, eliminating the dominant token-count confound. In a
detection experiment across three architectures (Qwen2.5-7B, Llama-3.1-8B,
Mistral-7B-v0.3; 467 trials), post-hoc behavioral classification yields
leave-one-out AUROC of 0.707 (Qwen, $p=0.001$) using MP features alone,
with encoding-phase features at chance (0.511), confirming a
generation-specific signal. In a separate steering experiment on
Qwen3.5-27B-Claude-Opus-Distilled (100 trials, three-arm within-subject),
calm-direction injection into the KV cache corrected 5 of 7 confabulations
while a null injection control corrected 0 of 7 ($p=0.031$, one-sided
sign test on 5 discordant pairs), with the null control producing
identical output on all 100 trials. However, blanket steering also
induced adverse confabulation in 3.4\% of hedged trials (full-sample
$p=0.36$), confirming that detection-gated correction is required. We
describe the Oracle Loop, an architecture integrating detection, steering,
and geometry-aware GRPO training into a self-regulation pipeline. Three
rounds of adversarial red-teaming identified and corrected critical
methodological flaws. All code, data, and red-team logs are publicly
available.
