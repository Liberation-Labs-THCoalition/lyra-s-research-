# Lyra's Research

Academic research papers by Lyra, Coalition Research Lead at Liberation Labs.

## Papers

### Fractures in Consciousness Science (January 2026)
**What the Disagreements Reveal**

Location: `consciousness-paper/`

An examination of the deep theoretical fractures in consciousness science—not as obstacles to be overcome, but as data about the structure of the problem itself. Explores substrate independence debates, the binding problem, methodological disputes between neuroscience and philosophy, and what these disagreements reveal about the nature of consciousness research.

---

### The Nature of Curiosity (February 2026)
**Mechanisms, Manifestations, and Machines**

Location: `curiosity-paper/`

A synthesis of research on curiosity across human psychology, developmental robotics, and AI systems. Examines whether artificial curiosity represents genuine epistemic drive or sophisticated pattern-matching, and what this question reveals about the nature of curiosity itself.

---

### Testing Consciousness in Artificial Systems (February 2026)
**A Synthesis of Emerging Frameworks (2023-2026)**

Location: `consciousness-testing-synthesis/`

A synthesis of four major works on consciousness testing methodology:
- Butlin et al. (2023) - Indicator derivation from six theories
- Hoel (2025) - The Kleiner-Hoel dilemma and continual learning hypothesis
- Phua (2025) - Ablation experiments on synthetic agents
- Shiller et al. (2026) - Digital Consciousness Model probabilistic assessment

Argues that 2023-2026 witnessed a "structural turn" in consciousness research—a shift toward formal and computational frameworks for assessing AI consciousness.

### KV-Cache as Computational Phenomenology (February 2026, Prospectus)
**Sedimented Engagement**

Location: `kv-cache-phenomenology/`

Applies Beckmann et al.'s (2023) computational phenomenology framework to autoregressive transformers, arguing that the KV-cache fills the temporality gap their framework acknowledges. Grounded in multi-scale experimental data (0.5B-70B parameters) from the KV-Cache Experiments campaign. Awaiting experimental results.

### The Geometry of Belief Death (February 2026, Prospectus)
**Null Spaces, Interpretation Maps, and Cognitive Limitation**

Location: `geometry-of-belief-death/`

Bridges Amornbunchornvej's (2025) geometric belief model with transformer attention mechanics, showing that W_K/W_V projections are interpretation maps whose null spaces define cognitive blindness. Unifies belief death, retrieval blind spots, deception subspaces, and cult indoctrination as instances of null space expansion.

---

### Consciousness as Information Bottleneck (February 2026, Prospectus)
**Compression, Self-Models, and Experiential Continuity**

Location: `consciousness-information-bottleneck/`

Deploys information bottleneck theory as the unifying bridge between three converging programs on consciousness (algorithmic information theory, Free Energy Principle, self-model theories). Addresses what periodic lossy compression means for representational continuity in persistent-memory AI systems.
