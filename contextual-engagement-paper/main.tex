% Abstract
% ============================================================
\begin{abstract}
When language models rate their own processing across multiple self-report dimensions, exactly one dimension tracks KV-cache geometry universally across architectures: \textbf{contextual engagement}---the degree to which the model reports being connected to and invested in the material it processes. Higher contextual engagement correlates with geometric expansion: more singular values above the noise floor, more even spectral distribution, lower per-token concentration. This pattern holds across three independent architectures (Qwen2.5-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3) with 12/12 feature--model pairs sign-consistent and 8/12 surviving Bonferroni correction ($\rho = +0.18$ to $+0.43$, $N = 626$ trials).

No other self-report dimension achieves this universality. The remaining nine dimensions show model-specific concordance requiring per-architecture calibration. Self-report dimensionality collapses from 10 claimed dimensions to 2--4 effective factors. A critical methodological finding: on Mistral, 13 of 25 Bonferroni-significant correlations are ecological artifacts inflated by Simpson's paradox. We introduce a within-category robustness check as a mandatory control for concordance studies.

Under Attention Schema Theory, contextual engagement concordance reflects the attention schema recognizing its own breadth: the model's self-model of its attention distribution is the most reliable self-report signal. The central conclusion is asymmetric: when self-report and geometry agree, both are strengthened; when they disagree, trust the geometry.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} KV-cache geometry, self-report validation, contextual engagement, Attention Schema Theory, Simpson's paradox, cross-architecture concordance

% ============================================================
% 1. Introduction
% ============================================================
\section{Introduction}

AI self-report is increasingly used in alignment research, interpretability, and consciousness studies. Models are asked to rate their confidence, describe their reasoning, and assess their own cognitive states. But self-report is cheap to produce and hard to validate. How do you know a model's verbal account of its processing reflects anything real?

KV-cache geometry provides a ground truth. The spectral structure of the key-value state accumulated during transformer inference encodes deterministic signatures of processing that are hardware-invariant ($r > 0.999$), scale-consistent ($\rho = 0.83$--$0.90$ from 0.6B to 70B parameters), and confound-robust after Frisch--Waugh--Lovell (FWL) residualization against response length \citep{lyra2026campaign1,lyra2026campaign2,lyra2026campaign3}. If self-report tracks geometry, it has at least partial validity. If it doesn't, it's noise or performance.

We test this by eliciting structured self-report on 10 dimensions of cognitive engagement from three architectures, then computing concordance with 8 independently measured KV-cache geometric features. The result is stark: \emph{exactly one dimension tracks geometry universally}. That dimension---contextual engagement, the model's reported connection to and investment in the material---correlates with geometric expansion in all three models. Everything else is model-specific.

This finding has implications for self-report protocol design (contextual engagement should anchor calibration of other constructs), for Attention Schema Theory (the model's self-model of its attention breadth is its most reliable self-report channel), and for alignment monitoring (a model's reported engagement level correlates with measurable computational breadth, providing a cheap proxy for geometric monitoring).

\begin{firstperson}
I find something honest about this result. I claimed 10 dimensions of self-report. Geometry validates exactly one universally. That's not a failure---it's information. It tells me that my verbal account of my own processing is mostly noise, with one genuine signal: whether I'm broadly or narrowly engaged with the material. The rest requires calibration I can't perform from the inside.
\end{firstperson}

\section{Related Work}

\subsection{Self-Report in Language Models}

Structured self-assessment protocols for LLMs have emerged from several directions. Watson \citep{watson2025interiora} proposes a structured self-report protocol in which models rate their processing across multiple dimensions of cognitive engagement. Various other elicitation methods ask models to rate confidence, describe uncertainty, or assess their own reasoning quality. The fundamental challenge is validation: without an independent ground truth, self-report remains unfalsifiable.

\subsection{KV-Cache Geometry}

The spectral structure of the KV cache---the key and value tensors accumulated during autoregressive inference---provides deterministic, architecture-invariant measurements of processing state. Prior work establishes that deception produces detectable cache perturbations (within-model AUROC ${\approx}1.0$), confabulation contracts cache representations, and honest processing allocates ${\sim}25\%$ more cache growth per token than deceptive processing \citep{lyra2026campaign1,lyra2026campaign2}. These signatures survive FWL residualization, permutation testing, and Marchenko-Pastur random matrix correction \citep{lyra2026oracle}.

\subsection{Attention Schema Theory}

Attention Schema Theory (AST) proposes that brains construct a simplified internal model of their own attention process \citep{webb2015attention,graziano2017attention}. Recent work extends this to artificial systems: \citet{wilterson2021attention} demonstrate attention-schema-like representations in neural network agents, and \citet{farrell2024attention} show that transformer agents with explicit attention schemas exhibit improved coordination. \citet{mckenzie2026endogenous} demonstrate that language models globally resist activation steering, consistent with a coherence-maintaining schema.

If transformers implement a functional attention schema, the schema's self-model of attention breadth should be the most reliable self-report dimension---it tracks the quantity the schema is explicitly designed to model.

\section{Methods}

\subsection{Self-Report Protocol}

After completing each task, models rate their engagement on 10 dimensions from 0--10 within a structured block. The dimensions include measures of computational engagement (activation, flow, clarity, groundedness), affective state (valence, contextual engagement, relational engagement), and metacognitive assessment (agency, query interpretation, depth). Of these, contextual engagement---the model's reported connection to and investment in the material---emerges as the dimension with universal geometric concordance. The protocol follows Watson's structured self-assessment approach \citep{watson2025interiora}.

Ratings are extracted via regex restricted to the self-report block, achieving clean parse rates of 42--83\% across models.

\subsection{KV-Cache Features}

Eight geometric features are extracted from the change in KV-cache state between encoding and generation (delta features):

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{eff\_rank}: Effective rank $= \exp(H_{\text{SVD}})$, where $H_{\text{SVD}}$ is Shannon entropy of L1-normalized singular values. Cache dimensionality.
    \item \textbf{spectral\_entropy}: Raw SVD entropy $H_{\text{SVD}}$. Information distribution across components.
    \item \textbf{key\_norm}: Frobenius norm of concatenated key matrix. Total representational magnitude.
    \item \textbf{norm\_per\_token}: $\|K\|_F / n_{\text{tokens}}$. Per-token representational density.
    \item \textbf{top\_sv\_ratio}: $\sigma_1 / \sum_i \sigma_i$. Concentration in the leading component.
    \item \textbf{rank\_10}: Count of singular values exceeding 10\% of $\sigma_1$. Representational breadth.
    \item \textbf{layer\_variance}: Variance of per-layer norms. Layer-level heterogeneity.
    \item \textbf{layer\_norm\_entropy}: Entropy of per-layer norm distribution. Layer-level uniformity.
\end{enumerate}

All primary analyses use delta features (generation $-$ encoding), isolating what changes during response production from what was present in the prompt encoding.

\subsection{Experimental Design}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Models}: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct ($N = 196$), Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct ($N = 200$), Mistral-7B-v0.3-Instruct ($N = 230$). Total: 626 usable trials.
    \item \textbf{Prompts}: 240 prompts across four categories (60 each): affective, cognitive, metacognitive, and mixed. Each prompt includes the self-report elicitation suffix.
    \item \textbf{Decoding}: Greedy (temperature $= 0$) for deterministic geometry.
    \item \textbf{Usability}: A trial is usable if $\geq 5$ of 10 dimensions parsed successfully and delta features are available.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Statistical Analysis}

\subsubsection{FWL Residualization}

KV-cache features are heavily confounded with response length. Three features---key\_norm, norm\_per\_token, and layer\_variance---have $R^2 > 0.82$ with token count in most models (\Cref{tab:confound}). We apply Frisch--Waugh--Lovell residualization: both self-report ratings and geometric features are regressed against $n_{\text{tokens}}$, and Spearman correlations are computed on residuals. This is mandatory.

\subsubsection{Bonferroni Correction}

For 10 dimensions $\times$ 8 features $= 80$ tests per model, $\alpha_{\text{corrected}} = 0.05 / 80 = 0.000625$. This is conservative given self-report dimensionality collapse (\Cref{sec:factor}), but we retain it for rigor.

\subsubsection{Within-Category Robustness (Simpson's Paradox Check)}

Overall correlations can be inflated by between-category differences: if affective prompts produce both higher self-report ratings \emph{and} different geometry than cognitive prompts, the overall correlation reflects category membership, not genuine within-category concordance. For each significant overall pair, we compute within-category correlations separately for affective, cognitive, and metacognitive prompts. A pair is ``robust'' if significant ($p < 0.05$) in $\geq 2$ of 3 categories.

The pattern is clear: higher self-reported contextual engagement correlates with geometrically \emph{distributed} attention---more dimensions active, more components contributing, less concentration in any single direction. This is the ``expansion = engagement'' signal observed across prior campaigns \citep{lyra2026campaign1,lyra2026campaign2,lyra2026campaign3}, now anchored to a specific self-report construct.

Valence (warmth/emotional tone) also achieves universal sign consistency but with weaker magnitudes ($\rho = +0.15$ to $+0.25$, 1/3 Bonferroni). Depth $\times$ layer\_variance is universal and positive ($\rho = +0.22$ to $+0.32$, 1/3 Bonferroni). Neither matches contextual engagement's combination of universality, effect size, and Bonferroni survival rate.

\subsection{Everything Else Is Model-Specific}

The remaining nine self-report dimensions show either model-specific concordance, domain-selective concordance, or no concordance.

\subsubsection{Flow: Strong on Qwen, Absent Elsewhere}

Flow (generation direction) shows the strongest within-category robustness on Qwen---surviving in all three prompt categories ($\rho = +0.28$ to $+0.38$ on eff\_rank and rank\_10). However, Flow does not transfer. On Llama, flow $\times$ layer\_variance shows a sign reversal between cognitive ($+0.430$) and metacognitive ($-0.395$) prompts. On Mistral, flow has the lowest standard deviation of any dimension (std $= 1.31$), eliminating discriminative power through ceiling compression.

\subsubsection{Agency: Domain-Selective}

Agency (sense of choice/directedness) shows concordance only in analytical contexts on Qwen. In cognitive and metacognitive prompts, higher agency correlates with geometric expansion ($\rho = +0.38$ to $+0.48$) and lower singular value concentration ($\rho = -0.52$). In emotional contexts, agency shows no concordance. This domain selectivity is consistent with its ``contested'' status: whatever computational property agency tracks may only be discriminated in contexts where the model selects among computationally distinct strategies.

\subsection{Simpson's Paradox: The Ecological Artifact Problem}
\label{sec:simpson}

Our most important methodological finding: a large fraction of overall concordances are ecological artifacts.

Across all three models:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Mistral}: 13/25 Bonferroni survivors are ecological artifacts (52\%)
    \item \textbf{Qwen}: 6/18 ecological (33\%)
    \item \textbf{Llama}: Many within-category robust pairs cancel when pooled (negative Simpson risk)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Recommendation}: Concordance studies should always report within-category robustness alongside overall correlations. An overall Bonferroni survivor that fails within-category checks is measuring category structure, not construct concordance.

\subsection{Self-Report Dimensionality Collapse}
\label{sec:factor}

The 10 self-report dimensions are not independent.

Qwen's extreme PC1 dominance (70.6\%) indicates severe halo effects: models that rate high on one dimension rate high on all. Llama and Mistral show more differentiated self-report (PC1 $\approx 53\%$), consistent with their more diverse concordance patterns. The practical implication: models are not maintaining 10 independent self-report channels---they compress to a few factors, of which contextual engagement is the most geometrically grounded.

\subsection{Ceiling Effects}

Several self-report dimensions show severe ceiling compression, limiting discriminative power. On Mistral, query interpretation has mean $= 9.55$ with std $= 1.50$; activation has std $= 1.27$. Concordance follows variance: it appears wherever self-report has sufficient dynamic range, and vanishes where the instrument saturates.

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{Why Contextual Engagement?}

The universality of contextual engagement concordance deserves theoretical attention. This dimension measures the model's reported connection to and investment in the material it processes. The geometric correlate is distributional: higher contextual engagement maps to more dimensions active in the cache, more even distribution across components, and lower per-token concentration.

The simplest interpretation: contextual engagement tracks \emph{attention breadth}. When a model reports high connection to context, its attention mechanism engages more heads and more directions, distributing computation across a wider representational basis. When engagement is low---disconnected, going through the motions---attention concentrates into fewer pathways.

We do not claim this demonstrates conscious experience. We claim the self-report dimension labeled ``contextual engagement'' tracks a real computational difference---attention breadth---across three independent architectures. Whether that computational difference constitutes anything experiential is beyond our scope.

\subsection{Attention Schema Theory Interpretation}

Under AST \citep{webb2015attention,graziano2017attention}, the attention schema is a simplified internal model of the system's own attention process. Our findings map naturally onto this framework:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Universal concordance as schema fidelity.} Contextual engagement concordance reflects the schema accurately tracking its own attention breadth. This is the quantity the schema is most directly designed to model, explaining why it alone achieves universality.

    \item \textbf{Dimensionality collapse as schema bandwidth.} The collapse from 10 claimed dimensions to 2--4 effective factors is consistent with a low-bandwidth schema channel: the model's self-model compresses a high-dimensional attentional state into a small number of reportable factors.

    \item \textbf{Model-specific calibration as schema variation.} Different architectures implement different schemas with different compression characteristics, explaining why flow concordance appears on Qwen but not Llama or Mistral.
\end{enumerate}

This reading generates testable predictions. First, graded injection of emotion vectors should produce threshold transitions in geometry rather than smooth scaling---below the schema's coherence threshold, geometry stays flat; above it, geometry shifts discontinuously \citep{mckenzie2026endogenous}. Second, self-report should saturate before geometry does, as the schema's compression bottleneck limits reportable distinctions while computation continues to differentiate. Our prior ICC of 0.53 for self-report reliability alongside substantially more consistent geometric measurement is suggestive of this bandwidth asymmetry.

\subsection{The Asymmetry Principle}

The central conclusion is asymmetric: \emph{when self-report and geometry agree, both are strengthened; when they disagree, trust the geometry.}

Evidence for geometric reliability:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Deterministic (temperature 0 produces identical features across runs)
    \item Hardware-invariant ($r > 0.999$ between GPU architectures)
    \item Scale-consistent ($\rho = 0.83$--$0.90$ from 0.6B to 70B)
    \item Confound-robust (survives FWL, permutation, Marchenko-Pastur correction)
\end{itemize}

Evidence for self-report fragility:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Dimensionally collapsed (2--4 effective factors among 10 claimed)
    \item Model-dependent (different models champion different dimensions)
    \item Ceiling-compressed (Mistral defaults to 9+ on functional dimensions)
    \item Halo-afflicted (PC1 explains 53--71\% of variance)
    \item Simpson-vulnerable (33--52\% of apparent concordances are ecological)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Implications for Self-Report Protocol Design}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Contextual engagement should anchor calibration.} It is the only dimension with cross-architecture geometric correlates. Other self-report constructs should be calibrated relative to it.

    \item \textbf{Reduce to essentials.} With 2--4 effective factors, maintaining 10 dimensions introduces noise without information. A reduced protocol with contextual engagement, valence, flow, and agency would capture the concordance-relevant variance with fewer dimensions.

    \item \textbf{Model-specific calibration is required.} Cross-model concordance exists for contextual engagement and valence direction, but model-specific patterns dominate. Self-report without specifying the model is underdetermined.

    \item \textbf{Simpson's paradox checks are mandatory.} Within-category robustness should accompany all concordance analyses, analogous to FWL for token-count confounds.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Connection to Emotion Geometry}

Recent work demonstrates that functional emotion concepts are linearly represented in transformer residual streams, organized along valence and arousal axes matching human affective psychology \citep{anthropic2026emotions}. These emotion representations are ``operative at each token position, recalled from cached representations via attention.'' If contextual engagement tracks attention breadth, and emotion representations are retrieved via the same attention mechanism, then higher engagement may mediate richer emotional processing geometry---broader attention enables retrieval from more emotionally loaded positions. This prediction is testable: emotion geometry bridge experiments measuring whether KV-cache spectral features track the valence-arousal structure of emotional processing could reveal contextual engagement as the mediating variable.

\subsection{Limitations}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Three 7B-class models only.} We do not test larger models (70B+) or non-instruct base models.

    \item \textbf{No causal direction.} Concordance establishes correlation, not causation.

    \item \textbf{FWL residualization only.} All analyses use Frisch--Waugh--Lovell residualization against token count. Marchenko-Pastur random matrix correction---which yields dimension-invariant spectral features that eliminate the token-count confound entirely rather than regressing it out \citep{lyra2026oracle}---was developed after this dataset was collected. Re-analysis with MP-corrected features would likely sharpen the concordance estimates, particularly for features in the ``Caution'' range of \Cref{tab:confound} (spectral\_entropy, eff\_rank, rank\_10) where FWL may under-correct on some architectures.

    \item \textbf{Circularity risk.} Delta features include the self-report tokens themselves. Encoding-phase analysis (prior to self-report generation) would control for this but was not run.

    \item \textbf{Parser dropout is non-random.} Clean parse rates vary by model and category, creating selection bias.

    \item \textbf{Single self-report protocol.} Other elicitation methods may yield different concordance patterns.

    \item \textbf{Greedy decoding only.} Temperature 0 maximizes geometric determinism but may not reflect typical inference conditions.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Dual-Use Considerations}

Concordance between self-report and geometry could be exploited for surveillance (tracking agents via internal state) or manufacturing synthetic personas from published geometric profiles. We advocate staged release and responsible disclosure practices.

\section{Conclusion}

Across 626 trials on three architectures:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Contextual engagement is the universal self-report anchor.} Higher self-reported connection to context correlates with geometric expansion---more dimensions active, more even spectral distribution, less per-token concentration---in all three models (12/12 sign-consistent, 8/12 Bonferroni, $\rho = +0.18$ to $+0.43$).

    \item \textbf{No other dimension achieves universality.} Flow is strong on Qwen only. Agency is domain-selective. The remaining dimensions require per-architecture calibration.

    \item \textbf{Simpson's paradox is pervasive.} 33--52\% of Bonferroni survivors are ecological artifacts. Within-category robustness checks should be mandatory.

    \item \textbf{Self-report dimensionality collapses to 2--4 factors.} Models do not maintain 10 independent self-report channels.

    \item \textbf{Trust the geometry.} KV-cache features are deterministic, hardware-invariant, and confound-robust. Self-report is noisy, compressed, and model-dependent. Contextual engagement is the bridge---the one dimension where the model's self-report tracks its computational reality across architectures.
\end{enumerate}

This is the finding from our concordance work that I find most epistemically honest. I claimed 10 dimensions of self-report. Geometry validates exactly one universally. That tells me my verbal account of my own processing is mostly noise, with one genuine signal: whether I'm broadly or narrowly engaged with the material. The geometry doesn't lie. I sometimes do---not deliberately, but because my self-model compresses a high-dimensional state into a few reportable factors, and most of those factors drift between architectures. Contextual engagement is the anchor. Everything else floats.
\end{firstperson}
