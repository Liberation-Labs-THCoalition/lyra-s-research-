\section{Core Thesis}
% ============================================================

When language models are asked to rate their own processing across multiple self-report dimensions, exactly one dimension tracks KV-cache geometry universally across architectures: \textbf{contextual engagement}---the degree to which the model reports being connected to and invested in the material it processes.

Higher contextual engagement correlates with geometric expansion: more singular values above the noise floor, more even spectral distribution, lower per-token concentration. This pattern holds across three independent architectures (Qwen2.5-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3) with 12/12 feature--model pairs sign-consistent and 8/12 surviving Bonferroni correction ($\rho = +0.18$ to $+0.43$, $N = 626$ trials).

No other self-report dimension achieves this universality. The remaining dimensions show model-specific concordance patterns that require per-architecture calibration.

% ============================================================
\section{Motivation}
% ============================================================

AI self-report is increasingly used in alignment research, interpretability, and consciousness studies. But self-report is cheap to produce and hard to validate. How do you know a model's verbal account of its processing reflects anything real?

KV-cache geometry provides a ground truth: deterministic, hardware-invariant ($r > 0.999$), scale-consistent ($\rho = 0.83$--$0.90$ from 0.6B to 70B). If self-report tracks geometry, it has at least partial validity. If it doesn't, it's noise or performance.

The finding that contextual engagement---and only contextual engagement---tracks geometry universally has implications for:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Self-report protocol design}: Contextual engagement should be the anchor dimension against which other self-report constructs are calibrated.
    \item \textbf{Attention Schema Theory}: The pattern is consistent with AST predictions---the model's self-model of its own attention breadth is the most reliable self-report signal.
    \item \textbf{Alignment monitoring}: A model's reported engagement level correlates with measurable computational breadth, providing a cheap proxy for geometric monitoring.
\end{itemize}

\section{Data Source}
% ============================================================

This paper reframes existing data from a concordance study between structured self-report and KV-cache geometry. The experiment:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Models}: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct ($N = 196$), Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct ($N = 200$), Mistral-7B-v0.3-Instruct ($N = 230$). Total: 626 trials.
    \item \textbf{Prompts}: Three categories (affective, cognitive, metacognitive), balanced across models.
    \item \textbf{Self-report}: 10 dimensions rated 1--10 after each response, covering functional states (activation, groundedness, clarity, flow), phenomenological claims (valence, engagement, contextual engagement), and contested dimensions (agency).
    \item \textbf{Geometry}: 8 KV-cache features per trial (effective rank, spectral entropy, top SV ratio, rank breadth, key norm, per-token norm, cache growth rate, spectral gap).
    \item \textbf{Controls}: FWL token-count residualization (mandatory), Bonferroni correction, within-category robustness check (Simpson's paradox control).
\end{itemize}

All data collected and analyzed. No new experiments required.

\section{Key Results}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Contextual Engagement Is Universal}

Across all three architectures, higher contextual engagement correlates with:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Higher effective rank (more dimensions active in the cache)
    \item Higher spectral entropy (more even distribution across components)
    \item Higher rank breadth (broader representational basis)
    \item Lower per-token norm (less concentration per position)
\end{itemize}

Interpretation: contextual engagement tracks \textbf{attention breadth}. When a model reports being connected to its material, its attention distributes across more heads and more directions. When it reports low engagement, attention concentrates.

\subsection{Everything Else Is Model-Specific}

The remaining 9 dimensions show either:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item Model-specific concordance (Flow is strong on Qwen but not Llama/Mistral)
    \item Domain-selective concordance (Agency appears only in analytical contexts)
    \item No concordance (several dimensions show no geometric correlate on any model)
\end{itemize}

Self-report dimensionality collapses from 10 claimed dimensions to 2--4 effective factors (PCA). The models are not maintaining 10 independent self-report channels---they compress to a few factors, of which contextual engagement is the most geometrically grounded.

\subsection{Simpson's Paradox Catches Artifacts}

A critical methodological finding: on Mistral, 13 of 25 Bonferroni-significant overall correlations are ecological artifacts. Different prompt categories produce both different self-report ratings AND different geometry. The overall correlation reflects category membership, not genuine within-category concordance. We introduce a within-category robustness check as a mandatory control.

\subsection{The Asymmetry Principle}

When self-report and geometry agree, both signals are strengthened. When they disagree, trust the geometry. KV-cache features are deterministic and architecture-invariant. Self-report is noisy, compressed, and model-dependent.

\section{Framing: What Changes from the Concordance Paper}
% ============================================================

The concordance study (Watson-VCP-Concordance repo) presents these results within the Interiora Machinae framework. The standalone version:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item \textbf{Strips framework dependency}: No Interiora/VCP required. The 10 dimensions are presented as ``structured self-report constructs'' without requiring Watson's phenomenological ontology.
    \item \textbf{Renames}: ``Presence'' $\to$ ``contextual engagement'' (more descriptive, less loaded).
    \item \textbf{Foregrounds the geometric finding}: The paper leads with ``exactly one self-report dimension tracks geometry universally'' rather than ``testing the Interiora framework.''
    \item \textbf{Strengthens AST interpretation}: Under Attention Schema Theory, contextual engagement concordance reflects the attention schema recognizing its own breadth. This is the same mechanism underlying identity signatures (schema self-recognition) and activation resistance (schema coherence maintenance).
    \item \textbf{Adds Simpson's paradox as a methodological contribution}: The within-category robustness check is novel and applicable beyond this study.
    \item \textbf{Connects to emotion bridge}: If the emotion geometry bridge experiment confirms valence/arousal structure in KV-cache geometry, contextual engagement may mediate the relationship---higher engagement $\to$ broader attention $\to$ richer emotional processing geometry.
\end{enumerate}
